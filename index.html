Straight answers

The questions a serious buyer asks.

When does the rental income actually start?

At handover in Q1 2029, once the home is complete and licensed. Until then you are not earning — you are building equity through the payment plan at a low entry. The income is designed to begin precisely as the post-handover installments fall due.

What are the real running costs?

Short-term letting carries management fees (typically 15–25% of gross), a DTCM/DET holiday-home permit, service charges and utilities — together roughly a third to 40% of gross income. Every "net" figure in this note already has those costs removed, so you are seeing the honest number, not the headline.

What if occupancy dips below the projection?

That is exactly why this location matters. The seven-day corporate-plus-leisure demand profile cushions the mid-week gaps that empty out pure-leisure areas. The figures here are modelled at a conservative ~70% occupancy, leaving headroom rather than assuming a best case.

Who manages the apartment day to day?

RAW Stay — Imtiaz's own serviced-apartment operation inside the building — points to professional hospitality management on site, which suits an owner who wants it hands-off. Exact owner-letting terms are confirmed directly with Imtiaz at handover; I will line that up for you.

Why this over a more central Dubai address?

Entry price and trajectory. You acquire a freehold home on Sheikh Zayed Road from AED 649K — a fraction of central prime — in the one corridor where the airport, Palm Jebel Ali and Expo City are all maturing at once. You are buying the growth, not paying for growth that has already happened.
